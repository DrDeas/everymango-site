Features

What is Brain Dump and how do I use it?

Brain Dump is your mental catch-all. It's the orange bar at the top of the screen where you can instantly capture any thought, task, or idea.

How to use it:

  • Type your thought in the Brain Dump bar
  • Press Enter or click "Capture"
  • Your thought is saved instantly
  • Organize it later when you have bandwidth

The key insight: capture now, organize later. Don't let ideas slip away while deciding where they "belong."

What is Focus Mode?

Focus Mode hides everything except your starred (priority) tasks. When you're feeling overwhelmed by a long task list, Focus Mode cuts through the noise.

To use it:

  • Star the tasks that matter most right now
  • Toggle on Focus Mode
  • Only your starred tasks will be visible

It's like putting blinders on â€” intentionally limiting your view so you can actually focus.

How do Quick Wins work?

Quick Wins filters your task list to show only tasks that take 15 minutes or less. It's perfect for:

  • Low-energy days â€” when big tasks feel impossible
  • Building momentum â€” small wins create dopamine hits
  • Using spare time â€” got 10 minutes? Knock something out

To use it, just set an estimated duration on your tasks, then toggle the Quick Wins filter.

What is Gentle Mode (No-Judgment Mode)?

Gentle Mode changes how EveryMango talks about overdue tasks. Instead of guilt-inducing language like "5 days overdue," you'll see softer phrasing like "5 days ago â€” no judgment!"

Why? Because beating yourself up never helped anyone get things done. The task is still there; we just don't need to rub it in.

You can toggle Gentle Mode on or off in your settings based on your preference.

How do Energy Levels work?

Energy Levels let you tag tasks by how much mental or physical energy they require: Low, Medium, or High.

Why it matters: On a low-spoons day, you can filter for low-energy tasks. On a high-energy day, tackle the big stuff. Match your work to how you actually feel.

Examples:

  • Low energy: filing, email replies, organizing
  • Medium energy: research, routine meetings
  • High energy: creative work, difficult conversations, complex decisions

What is Someday/Maybe?

Someday/Maybe is a parking lot for ideas and tasks that aren't ready yet. They're not forgotten â€” they're just ripening.

Use it for:

  • Ideas you want to explore "someday"
  • Tasks that depend on something else happening first
  • Things you might want to do but aren't committing to yet

Moving items to Someday/Maybe removes them from your active view, reducing decision fatigue without losing them forever.

How does the Pomodoro Timer work?

The Pomodoro Timer helps you work in focused bursts. The classic technique is 25 minutes of work followed by a 5-minute break.

Why it works for ADHD:

  • Time constraints create urgency (good for initiation)
  • Knowing a break is coming makes starting easier
  • Built-in breaks prevent burnout

You can customize the work and break durations to fit your preferences.
